Statue of Liberty

Statue of Liberty, colossal statue on Liberty Island in the Upper New York Bay, U.S., commemorating the friendship of the peoples of the United States and France. Standing 305 feet (93 metres) high including its pedestal, it represents a woman holding a torch in her raised right hand and a tablet bearing the adoption date of the Declaration of Independence (July 4, 1776) in her left. The torch, which measures 29 feet (8.8 metres) from the flame tip to the bottom of the handle, is accessible via a 42-foot (12.8-metre) service ladder inside the arm (this ascent was open to the public from 1886 to 1916). An elevator carries visitors to the observation deck in the pedestal, which may also be reached by stairway, and a spiral staircase leads to an observation platform in the figure’s crown. A plaque at the pedestal’s entrance is inscribed with a sonnet, “The New Colossus” (1883) by Emma Lazarus.

How to Get There

From Manila, take a flight to New York City (JFK or Newark Airport) with a layover in cities like Tokyo, Dubai, or Doha. Upon arrival, travel to Lower Manhattan by taxi, airport shuttle, or subway. To visit the Statue of Liberty, go to Battery Park and take a ferry operated by Statue City Cruises to Liberty Island. The ferry ride includes a stop at Ellis Island, home to the historic immigration museum

Top Activities

  • Explore Liberty Island – Walk around the island and enjoy breathtaking views of the New York City skyline.
  • Visit the Statue of Liberty Museum – Learn about the statue’s history, construction, and significance.
  • Climb to the Crown – If you have a reserved ticket, ascend the narrow staircase to the crown for a unique view.
  • Tour Ellis Island – Discover the history of immigration in the U.S. at the Ellis Island Immigration Museum.
  • Take Stunning Photos – Capture iconic shots of the statue and the Manhattan skyline from various viewpoints.li>

Other Places to Visit

  • Ellis Island – Explore the Ellis Island Immigration Museum and learn about the millions of immigrants who arrived in the U.S. through this historic gateway.
  • One World Observatory – Enjoy panoramic views of New York City from the top of One World Trade Center.
  • Lucerne - Famous for its wooden bridges and medieval architectureBrooklyn Bridge – Walk or bike across this iconic bridge for stunning views of the skyline.
  • Times Square – Experience the bright lights, giant billboards, and the lively atmosphere of this famous entertainment hub.
  • Central Park – Relax in this vast green space, visit landmarks like Bethesda Fountain, or take a horse-drawn carriage ride.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit the Statue of Liberty is during the spring (April–June) and fall (September–November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller compared to peak summer months. Early mornings on weekdays are ideal to avoid long lines. Winter (December–February) offers fewer crowds but can be very cold, while summer (July–August) is the busiest and hottest time to visit.
