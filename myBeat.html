Here are my favorite pop tracks:

Kendrick Lamar - Luther

Genre: Pop

"Luther" is a testament to Kendrick Lamar's masterful storytelling and intricate lyricism. The song combines soulful melodies with introspective lyrics, reflecting on themes of struggle, perseverance, and self-discovery. Kendrick, a Pulitzer Prize-winning artist, is known for his ability to craft deeply layered narratives that resonate with listeners across generations. With its haunting production and compelling delivery, "Luther" stands as a powerful addition to Lamar's discography, solidifying his impact on the contemporary music scene.

Kendrick Lamar, SZA - All The Stars

Genre: Pop

This song, featured in the "Black Panther" soundtrack, is an uplifting anthem that blends R&B and hip-hop influences. Its cinematic production and powerful vocals from SZA complement Kendrick's sharp verses, making it a standout collaboration.

ROSE & Bruno Mars - APT

Genre: Pop

A soulful collaboration between ROSE and Bruno Mars, "APT" delivers smooth vocals and classic pop-funk instrumentation that showcases their individual strengths.

R&B

Here are my favorite R&B tracks:

So Sick - Ne-Yo

Genre: R&B

"So Sick" is a song by American singer-songwriter Ne-Yo, written alongside Norwegian production duo Stargate for Ne-Yo's debut studio album, In My Own Words (2006). Produced by Stargate, the song was released as the second single from the album on November 21, 2005, and received favorable reviews from music critics. "So Sick" peaked at number one on both the US Billboard Hot 100 and the UK Singles Chart. In Europe, the single topped the Eurochart Hot 100 and entered the top 20 in 10 countries, including Denmark, Hungary, Ireland, Norway, and Switzerland. In Australasia, the single reached number two in New Zealand and number four in Australia.

Dilemma - Nelly

Genre: R&B

"Dilemma" is a song by American rapper Nelly featuring American singer Kelly Rowland. It was released on June 25, 2002, as the second single from the former's second studio album, Nellyville (2002). The song also appeared on Rowland's debut solo studio album, Simply Deep (2002). An R&B ballad, the song consists of a high-pitched intonation and call and response, and contains a sample of Patti LaBelle's 1983 song "Love, Need and Want You". The lyrics describe a man infatuated with a woman committed in an existing relationship. "Dilemma" was written by Nelly, Antoine "Bam" Macon and Ryan Bowser, alongside the "Love, Need and Want You" writers, Kenneth Gamble and Bunny Sigler.
