Mt.Fuji

Mount Fuji, highest mountain in Japan. It rises to 12,388 feet (3,776 metres) near the Pacific Ocean coast in Yamanashi and Shizuoka ken (prefectures) of central Honshu, about 60 miles (100 km) west of the Tokyo-Yokohama metropolitan area. It is a volcano that has been dormant since its last eruption, in 1707, but is still generally classified as active by geologists. The mountain is the major feature of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park (1936), and it is at the centre of a UNESCO World Heritage site designated in 2013.

How to Get There

To get to Mount Fuji from the Philippines, fly from Manila (MNL), Cebu (CEB), or Clark (CRK) to Tokyo, Japan, via Narita (NRT) or Haneda (HND) Airport. From Tokyo, the fastest way is to take the JR Limited Express Fuji Excursion from Shinjuku Station to Kawaguchiko Station (~1 hr 50 min, ¥4,000/₱1,500). Another option is to take the Shinkansen (Bullet Train) from Tokyo Station to Mishima Station, then a bus to Kawaguchiko (~2 hrs, ¥6,000/₱2,200). The cheapest route is the Highway Bus from Shinjuku Station to Kawaguchiko Station (~2 hrs, ¥2,000/₱750). From Kawaguchiko Station, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Mount Fuji’s 5th Station (Yoshida Trail) for climbing or explore nearby attractions like Chureito Pagoda, Lake Kawaguchi, and Oshino Hakkai.

Top Activities

  • Hiking to the summit (July - September)
  • Visiting the Fuji Five Lakes
  • Exploring nearby hot springs (onsen)
  • Enjoying the scenic Chureito Pagoda view
  • Taking a boat cruise on Lake Ashi in Hakone

Other Places to Visit

  • Lake Kawaguchi – The most accessible of the Fuji Five Lakes, offering stunning reflections of Mount Fuji, scenic boat rides, and hot spring resorts.
  • Chureito Pagoda – A famous five-story pagoda with a breathtaking view of Mount Fuji, especially during cherry blossom season.
  • Hakone – A scenic hot spring town with beautiful nature, open-air museums, the Hakone Ropeway, and views of Mount Fuji from Lake Ashi.
  • Oshino Hakkai – A picturesque village with crystal-clear ponds, traditional thatched-roof houses, and fresh spring water from Mount Fuji.
  • Fuji-Q Highland – A thrilling amusement park with record-breaking roller coasters and rides, offering spectacular views of Mount Fuji.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mount Fuji depends on your activities. The official climbing season runs from July to early September when trails are open and the weather is mild. For sightseeing, late March to early April offers stunning cherry blossom views around Chureito Pagoda and Lake Kawaguchi, while October to November showcases vibrant autumn foliage. Winter (December to February) provides the clearest views of a snow-capped Mount Fuji, though climbing is not advisable due to extreme cold.
