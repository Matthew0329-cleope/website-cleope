Swiss Alps

The Alpine region of Switzerland, conventionally referred to as the Swiss Alps, represents a major natural feature of the country and is, along with the Swiss Plateau and the Swiss portion of the Jura Mountains, one of its three main physiographic regions. The Swiss Alps extend over both the Western Alps and the Eastern Alps, encompassing an area sometimes called Central Alps. While the northern ranges from the Bernese Alps to the Appenzell Alps are entirely in Switzerland, the southern ranges from the Mont Blanc massif to the Bernina massif are shared with other countries such as France, Italy, Austria and Liechtenstein.

How to Get There

To get to the Swiss Alps from the Philippines, fly from Manila (MNL) or Cebu (CEB) to major Swiss airports like Zurich (ZRH) or Geneva (GVA), usually with a layover in cities such as Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul. From Zurich or Geneva, take a scenic train ride to alpine destinations like Zermatt, Interlaken, or St. Moritz. The Swiss rail system, including routes like the Glacier Express and Bernina Express, offers breathtaking views of the Alps, making train travel the most convenient and picturesque way to reach the region.

Top Activities

  • Skiing and snowboarding in Zermatt and St. Moritz
  • Hiking in Jungfrau Region
  • Exploring historic villages like Grindelwald
  • Taking a cable car ride up the Matterhorn
  • Enjoying Swiss cuisine in alpine lodges

Other Places to Visit

  • Lake Geneva - A stunning lake surrounded by vineyards and historic towns
  • Interlaken - The adventure capital of Switzerland, offering paragliding and lake cruises
  • Lucerne - Famous for its wooden bridges and medieval architecture
  • Montreux - Known for its jazz festival and beautiful lakeside scenery
  • Matterhorn - One of the most iconic mountains in the world

Best Time to Visit

Winter (December - March) is ideal for skiing and snowboarding, while summer (June - September) is perfect for hiking and sightseeing.
