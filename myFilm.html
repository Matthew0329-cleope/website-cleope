Tv Shows and Movies

TV Shows

Title: Batang Quiapo

"Batang Quiapo" is a well-known title in Philippine entertainment, originating as a 1986 action-comedy film directed by Pablo Santiago, starring Fernando Poe Jr. and Maricel Soriano. The film was a box-office success and marked Poe's first project with Regal Films. In 2023, ABS-CBN released a television adaptation, "FPJ's Batang Quiapo", starring Coco Martin as Hesus Nazareno "Tanggol" A. Dimaguiba, a young man navigating life in the streets of Quiapo, Manila. The series explores themes of survival and identity, featuring an ensemble cast that includes Lovi Poe, Charo Santos-Concio, and Christopher de Leon. Directed by multiple filmmakers, including Martin himself, the series premiered on February 13, 2023, on Kapamilya Channel’s Primetime Bida and is also available for online streaming. The adaptation blends elements of the original film with a contemporary narrative, offering a portrayal of resilience and the struggles of common people in Quiapo.


Title: Pilipinas Got Talent

​"Pilipinas Got Talent" (PGT) is a premier Philippine reality television competition that showcases a diverse array of Filipino talents, from singing and dancing to unique and extraordinary performances. Adapted from the global "Got Talent" franchise created by Simon Cowell, PGT first aired on ABS-CBN on February 20, 2010, and has since crowned six winners over its initial six seasons. After a seven-year hiatus, the show is set to return for its seventh season on March 29, 2025, airing across multiple platforms including Kapamilya Channel, A2Z, and TV5. This season introduces a refreshed panel of judges featuring Freddie "FMG" Garcia, Donny Pangilinan, Eugene Domingo, and Kathryn Bernardo, bringing a blend of experience and fresh perspectives to the show. The hosting duties will be undertaken by Robi Domingo and Melai Cantiveros, adding their unique charm and energy to the program. Auditions for the upcoming season commenced in late 2024, with both online and physical auditions held to discover the nation's most exceptional talents. As PGT makes its much-anticipated comeback, it continues its mission to celebrate and elevate world-class Filipino talent on the national stage.


Title: World of Dance

"World of Dance" is an international dance competition franchise that originated in the United States. The American version premiered on NBC on May 30, 2017, featuring judges Jennifer Lopez, Derek Hough, and Ne-Yo, with contestants competing for a grand prize of $1 million. The show concluded after four seasons, with its cancellation announced in 2021. In the Philippines, "World of Dance Philippines" aired on ABS-CBN from January 12 to April 14, 2019. Hosted by Luis Manzano and Pia Wurtzbach, with online hosts AC Bonifacio, Maymay Entrata, and Riva Quenery, the show featured judges Billy Crawford, Maja Salvador, and Gary Valenciano. The competition was open to dancers aged 8 and above, both amateur and professional, across various styles. The inaugural season crowned FCPC Baliktanaw as the champion, with Luka & Jenalyn as runners-up and Ken San Jose in third place.

Movies

Title: Real Steel

​"Real Steel" is a 2011 American science fiction sports drama directed by Shawn Levy, featuring Hugh Jackman as Charlie Kenton, a former boxer navigating a future where robot boxing has become the dominant sport. The narrative centers on Charlie's unexpected journey with his estranged son, Max (Dakota Goyo), as they discover and train Atom, a discarded sparring robot, transforming him into a formidable contender in the ring. The film explores themes of redemption, family bonds, and the underdog spirit. Upon its release, "Real Steel" received praise for its compelling plot and character development, with critic Roger Ebert noting its genuine emotional engagement. The movie achieved commercial success, grossing nearly $300 million worldwide. For those interested in watching, "Real Steel" is available for streaming on platforms like Tubi. Additionally, discussions about a potential sequel and a television series adaptation have been reported, indicating the story's enduring appeal. ​


Title: The Karate Kid

"The Karate Kid" is a renowned martial arts film franchise that began with the 1984 movie directed by John G. Avildsen. The original film stars Ralph Macchio as Daniel LaRusso, a teenager who moves from New Jersey to Los Angeles and becomes the target of bullying by a group of karate students. He finds an unexpected mentor in Mr. Miyagi, portrayed by Pat Morita, who teaches him karate not only as a means of self-defense but also as a philosophy of life. The film was both a critical and commercial success, grossing over $130 million worldwide and earning Morita an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor. ​


Title: Avengers: Endgame

​"Avengers: Endgame" is a 2019 superhero film produced by Marvel Studios and directed by Anthony and Joe Russo. Serving as the culmination of the Marvel Cinematic Universe's (MCU) Infinity Saga, it follows the Avengers and their allies as they endeavor to reverse the catastrophic effects of Thanos's snap, which erased half of all life in the universe. The ensemble cast includes Robert Downey Jr., Chris Evans, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Hemsworth, Scarlett Johansson, and Jeremy Renner. The film was both a critical and commercial success, grossing nearly $2.8 billion worldwide and briefly holding the title of the highest-grossing film of all time. Notably, actress Elizabeth Olsen, who portrays Wanda Maximoff/Scarlet Witch in the MCU, revealed that she initially feared the film might underperform, despite its eventual monumental success.
